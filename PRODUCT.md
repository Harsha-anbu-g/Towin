# Product

## Register

product

(The `/` landing story and auth pages are brand surfaces; everything behind login is product. PRODUCT.md carries the product default — override per task for the landing/auth.)

## Users

- **Elders** — older adults seeking friendship, company, and help with daily tasks (shopping, a ride, someone to talk to). Often lower digital confidence, larger text needs, deliberate pace.
- **Helpers** — younger people offering time, energy, and company, earning money and a growing Trust Score.
- Context: used at home, unhurried, often on phones or older laptops. One task per sitting; nothing should rush or startle.

## Product Purpose

ToWin connects elders with trusted younger helpers nearby — "It takes two To Win." The core mechanic is **trust built slowly**: a visible Trust Score (max 15 points per connection: profile +3, seven-step Trust Ladder +7, reviews +5) and a mutual-consent ladder (Just Connected → Fully Trusted) that gates personal-info sharing. Success = elders feel safe enough to say yes, and helpers feel their reliability is seen and rewarded.

## Brand Personality

Calm · Patient · Trustworthy. The tortoise is the brand: slow and steady, one step at a time, trust grows like roots. Zen, meditative, warm, human — never corporate, busy, or sharp. Voice uses plain everyday words (elder-first); the tagline is exactly "It takes two To Win."

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated SaaS: bold sans hero + gradient accents + shadow-elevated card grids + uppercase tracked eyebrows everywhere.
- Dark-canvas "premium" tools (Linear/Mercury-style) — low-vision-hostile and off-brand.
- Loud/playful startup energy; anything urgent, gamified-aggressive, or youth-coded.
- Clinical/hospital sterility — calm must feel warm, not institutional.

## Design Principles

1. **One idea per screen.** Generous rhythm (64px sections); whitespace is part of the theme — let it breathe.
2. **Trust is visible and earned.** Trust mechanics (score, ladder, gold accents) always legible and honest — never decoration.
3. **Whisper, don't shout.** Serif headings at weight 400; restraint over emphasis; motion slow, soft, and justified.
4. **The main action is unmistakable.** Exactly one filled sky-blue primary per screen; everything else quiet (outlined/text).
5. **Elder-first always beats fashion.** If a trend reduces clarity for a 75-year-old, the trend loses.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Elderly-first: body text ≥16px (18px base), tap targets ≥44px, high contrast (body ≥4.5:1), clear field boundaries (no borderless inputs), filled primary buttons.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere (near-instant resolve, content never hidden).
- Keyboard: visible `:focus-visible` ring on all interactive elements; errors announced to screen readers (`role="alert"`).
- No dark mode for core surfaces; no light-grey-on-white body text.
